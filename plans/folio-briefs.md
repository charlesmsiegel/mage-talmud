# Folio briefs

One brief per planned folio. Each brief is a plan, not prose to paste:
everything on the eventual page is written fresh, in the project's voice,
under SPEC §0 (nothing reproduced from any book; Ein Mishpat cites pages
instead). Brief format: **Center** (Mishnah = law to state; Gemara = what to
derive/retell), **Disputes** (the outer commentary's named camps — a folio
with no real dispute is not ready to write), **LA** (Later Authorities
angle), **X** (cross-refs), **Hag** (hagahah seed). Cite M20 pages from the
ToC; the exact page for a specific rule may need checking against the book.

## Chapter I — The Nine Seats (2a ✅, 2b ✅, 3a ✅, 3b ✅)

### f003b — The Unseated Answer (daf) ✅
The verso the Masoret of 2a promised: the founding retold from outside.
**Center:** Mishnah — the Council's law of outsiders: parley rights, the
guest seat's protections, the status of the unseated (neither member nor
enemy), poaching and recruitment law. Gemara — a counter-aggada: the same
Convocation, told as the corralling; the crafts that walked out; centuries of
"Disparate" as an exonym; the modern alliance forming (kept brief — f197
carries the modern body).
**Disputes:** is the counter-telling a recension or a rival canon; whether
accepting a seat now would vindicate 1466 or repeat it; the exonym problem
(who names whom).
**LA:** playing Disparate PCs against Council NPCs who cite 2a at them; the
guest chair as a standing scene.
**X:** 2a (the corral reading), 197, the tenth seat. **Hag:** the losers of
an argument keep better minutes.

### f143 — Certámen (daf)
Duel law: trial by ordeal is native Talmudic territory.
**Center:** Mishnah — the duel as legal process: consent of both, agreed
stakes, a certified arbiter, the bounded circle, the yield; what may not be
staked. Gemara — origin among the Hermetic Houses, adoption by the Council
as the alternative to feud; a famous duel retold.
**Disputes:** is certámen justice or violence with paperwork; enforceability
of stakes across Traditions; dueling a superior; refusal and honor.
**LA:** running a duel as a scene (stakes on the table before dice); duels
as PvP pressure valve. **X:** 2a (Council law), f430 (mechanics — decide
merge question, see PLAN). **Hag:** the circle is the only place two
paradigms agree, and they agree to disagree formally.

## Chapter II — The Awakening

### f038 — Of Gods and Men (daf)
**Center:** Mishnah — the census of souls as law: Sleeper, Sleepwalker,
Awakened; what Awakening is (the Avatar rousing; the moment one notices the
world is negotiable); that it cannot be granted, only survived. Gemara —
the shapes Awakening takes, retold as three or four brief maasehs (the
mystic, the scientist, the accident, the seeker).
**Disputes:** can Awakening be induced (the Technocratic recruitment reading
vs. the mystic calling reading); mass awakening as goal or catastrophe.
**LA:** the prelude scene; Awakening as session one vs. backstory.
**X:** 533b (the census rhyme — say so in Masoret), f043. **Hag:** everyone
remembers their Awakening; no two accounts of the same one agree.

### f043 — The Avatar (daf)
**Center:** Mishnah — Avatar law: every mage has one; its rating bounds
Quintessence held and Seeking depth; the four Essences (Dynamic, Pattern,
Primordial, Questing) as temperaments of the daemon; Seekings as the road of
Arete; Gilgul as its destruction. Gemara — what the traditions say the
Avatar is, told as competing aggadot (shard of the divine; the higher self;
a passenger; the Genius of the Technocrat, denied and renamed).
**Disputes:** the identity question (self vs. other — if the Avatar wills
the Seeking, who passes it?); whether Essence is destiny or description.
**LA:** playing the Avatar as an NPC vs. as flavor; Seeking scenes.
**X:** 2b (Gilgul — the sentence presupposes this folio), f328, f533a (the
Avatar as internal witness). **Hag:** the Technocracy calls it Genius and
insists that is different; the Avatar does not mind what it is called.

### f046 — The Will to Power (daf)
**Center:** Mishnah — Arete as the measure of enlightened will; the cycle of
awareness, conflict, resolution as the law of a mage's growth; the Ascension
War defined (a war over what is real, fought mostly without battles).
Gemara — the stakes retold: what victory means to each side; why nobody has
won. **Disputes:** personal vs. cosmic Ascension (does one mage's rise move
the world?); whether the War metaphor damages what it names.
**LA:** chronicle stakes; the War as background radiation vs. active front.
**X:** 3a (the thesis: the law of who is watching), f168. **Hag:** the only
faction that thinks the War is over is losing it.

## Chapter III — The World

### f062 — Consensus and Belief (daf)
**Center:** Mishnah — the Consensus as law: reality is the sum of belief;
majorities bind; paradigm defined; local variation. Gemara — how consensus
shifted historically (retold, three beats: the mythic age, the burning, the
triumph of reason), deriving the present baseline.
**Disputes:** is the Consensus a fact about physics or about perception (the
strong and weak readings); who moved it — the Order's victory as conspiracy

**LA:** table baseline-reality declaration; reality zones preview.
**X:** 533a (this folio is its foundation — say so), f611 material folds in
here as a Later Authorities note on zones. **Hag:** the majority is always
sane by definition; the definition is the problem.

### f078 — The Life-Blood (daf)
**Center:** Mishnah — Quintessence law: the raw stuff of reality; held up to
Avatar rating; Tass as Quintessence embodied; Nodes as wells; ownership,
tithe, and trespass. Gemara — Node property law derived from custom:

**LA:** the chantry as home base; Node scarcity as campaign engine.

### f082 — The Gauntlet (daf)
**Center:** Mishnah — the veil between flesh and spirit as law: it thickens

**Disputes:** is the Gauntlet the Consensus's scar tissue or a made thing

**LA:** crossing as scene transition; stranding PCs. **X:** f091, f521.
**Hag:** the wall between worlds is measured in how hard the neighbors
disbelieve you.

## Chapter V — Avot: The Traditions (the f148 mold)

Mold (applies to all nine): **Mishnah** — the Tradition as law: its paradigm
in one clause, its practice, its common instruments, its seat (Sphere), its
own internal law (each Tradition has one; state it tersely). **Gemara** —
the chain of transmission, Pirkei Avot form: "X received from Y and handed
it to Z" — lineage retold from origin to the Convocation to now, with one
maaseh. **Inner** — the plain sense of playing one: what the paradigm
permits, how the practice shapes coincidence (ties to 533a). **Outer** —
the internal factions arguing (every Tradition contains its own dispute;
that dispute is the folio's engine). **LA** — chronicle use, stereotypes to
avoid, the Tradition's table pitfalls. **Hag** — earned, affectionate, dry. Names: the classic names are primary (Akashic Brotherhood, Cult of Ecstasy, Dreamspeakers, Virtual Adepts — the renames didn't take), except the Chakravanti and the Society of Ether, whose new names stuck; every folio carries its naming question as a live debate, never as a settled retitle.

### f148a — The Akashic Brotherhood (Mind) ✅
Internal law: the Akashic Record and who may read it; Do as practice-law.
Factions/dispute: warrior vs. scholar vs. merchant readings of the way
(Vajrapani / Jnani / Shi-Ren tension); whether Do may be taught outside.
X: f423 (Do mechanics), f519. Hag seed: the Record remembers every argument
about what the Record is.

### f148b — The Wheel (daf) ✅
The verso: the Akashic Brotherhood's law of return, per Lore of the Traditions.
**Center:** Mishnah — reincarnation law (near half of each generation are
returning souls; the sangha plans for it); the lùkŏu (karmic bond of two
Avatars across lifetimes, born of love or violent death, held until
resolved); teaching order (bodhimandala, the monastic first season, free
choice of school, senior/junior pairs). Gemara — a returning-soul maaseh;
the Wheel checks no affiliations (the Wu Lung arriving).
**Disputes:** does seniority survive death; the hateful lùkŏu as debt vs.
doom; the enemy reborn — welcome vs. infiltration (148a's gate dispute,
answered from above). **LA:** returning souls at the table (authorship
split, metering memory); the lùkŏu as troupe glue; past lives must never
drive. **X:** 148a, f043, 2b (Gilgul as the negative), 156.
**Hag:** half the sangha remembers dying for the Way.

### f150a — The Celestial Chorus (Prime) ✅
Internal law: the One sung by many; heresy and harmony procedure.
Dispute: the One as a being vs. the sum of the song (monotheist vs.
ecumenical readings); the Chorus's imperial history vs. its universalism.

### f152a — The Cult of Ecstasy (Time) ✅

### f154a — The Dreamspeakers (Spirit) ✅

### f156a — The Chakravanti (Entropy) ✅

### f158a — The Order of Hermes (Forces) ✅

### f162a — The Verbena (Life) ✅
