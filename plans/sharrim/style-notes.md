# Sharrim style notes — per-line interior styles

Body face for every line is Goudy Old Style (already embedded). Each entry
records the line's display face and the interior treatment observed from a
user-supplied page screenshot, to be translated into that tractate's shaar:
same arch-and-apparatus structure as Aliyah's shaar, re-skinned in the
line's own palette, display face, and ornament language.

## Masekha — Vampire: the Masquerade (V20)

- **Display face:** Delavan (`chrome/fonts/Delavan.TTF`, user-supplied).
- **Palette:** oxblood/dark maroon heads on a pale marbled grey-green
  ground; black structural ornament; body text near-black.
- **Frame/ornament:** wrought-iron fence border — black vertical bars with
  spearhead finials pointing in from top and bottom edges; inside it a thin
  double-rule frame with small square corner blocks. Gothic, funerary,
  architectural.
- **Heads:** major heads in Delavan, oxblood, large, with a thin rule
  beneath; sub-heads bold black serif, sentence-size. Cross-references set
  in the accent red.
- **Running matter:** grey footer band; page number left; chapter title
  centered in letterspaced small caps.
- **Register for the shaar:** aristocratic-legal; the Jyhad as endless
  litigation. Title page should feel like a deed to a haunted estate.
